About Jeremy Senh

Jeremy Senh is a 2nd year student in Drexel University majoring in User Experience and Interaction Design (UXID) at the Westphal College of Media Arts & Design. He is interested in working front-end in programming and designing websites, video games, apps, softwares, and more, and learn more about graphic design, developing front-end, and product design. He also likes to work with video editing and photography, and applying skills onto programs like DaVinci Resolve, Adobe Photoshop, and Figma.

Outside of class, he is a Game Lead for the Rhythm Game segment of the Drexel Gaming Association (DGA) where he has the responsibility to keep the members engaged and socializing by interacting on the club's Discord server, setting up events, and help out in the club's LANs (local area network) that they host every academic quarter.

He enjoys spending time with friends and family, likes playing arcade rhythm games, is an active member of the Fashion District Philly Friends (FDPF) community group where he has met many friends and is excited to meet many more to come, and working with singing vocal synthesizers like Vocaloid and UTAU whether it is creating his own voice banks or creating song covers with them. He had the honor to be interviewed and featured on the Philly Inquirer's story on FDPF. The story also has a video which was posted on social media beside their website.
